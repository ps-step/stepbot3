About StepBot3

This was completely vibe coded. I don't know how to do any of ts.

Features

  • Question Database: STEP 2 and 3 from 2008 to 2024. More questions will be added if I can be bothered, but this is unlikely.
  • Practice Mode: Filter questions by paper, type, and topic. You can track your progress in the sidebar, which filters based on selected topics.
  • Mock Mode: Generate timed mock papers consisting of 12 questions you have not done. Grade boundaries interpolated from years.
  • Progress Tracking: Marks questions as complete, logs your scores, and saves your notes automatically to your browser.
  • Mark Schemes: View PDF mark schemes within the app.

Note: All data is stored locally on your device. Clearing your browser cache will remove your progress.

Resources & Tips

A collection of useful resources for A Level, STEP, TMUA, MAT, and Interviews.

Edexcel A Level Maths

  • Past paper spam is the best way to revise maths. There are ridiculous amounts of past papers, both official ones and community ones. Use the PMT page for 2017+, but find more if needed. Do these in timed conditions.
  • Other sources include Crash Maths and Madas Maths.
  • Neil Does Maths is a niche channel going through past paper questions—good if you struggle to understand mark schemes.
  • A Level Maths Textbooks are actually good for learning the subject. Use Pete Hart's videos to accompany exercises.
  • YouTube Channels: TLMaths (comprehensive), Bicen Maths (great detail), and Haberdashers' Adams Maths.
  • Dr. Frost has comprehensive powerpoints, especially useful for mechanics.

Edexcel A Level Further Maths

  • Past paper spam again is the best method.
  • Textbooks (PDFs for Core Pure, FM1, FS1).
  • YouTube: FM Videos covers almost every exam board. Bicen Maths is also very complete.

TMUA, MAT, Senior Maths Challenge

  • R2Drew2 is the GOAT for TMUA videos and Senior Maths Challenge solutions.
  • TMUA Past Papers: Aim to do all official ones. Note that older papers are easier.
  • Community Papers:
    • Tyler Tutoring (Slightly easier)
    • Yotta (Diabolically hard)
    • Beyond Horizon (A touch harder)
    • STEPMaths (Two papers in MEGA folder)
    • 2024 Chinese Recalled Paper
  • MAT Past Papers are relevant for TMUA.
  • MAT Livestreams are the best official resource from Oxford.
  • Cambridge CS Applicant Discord has good resources (recommend lurking).

BMO

  • Past paper spam is the best way, but difficult given the 3.5h length.
  • Video Solutions are available for decades of questions.
  • Michael Penn covers many relevant Olympiad problems.

Oxbridge Interviews

  • The Ultimate Interview Drive: Contains mock interviews and past questions. Highly recommend the "Oxford interview questionnaires" and "Jesus College Specimen Admissions Test".
  • Useful Problem Sheets: "Oxbridge Maths Interview Questions" (Isaac Tseng), "TBO Problem Solving Booklet", "Curve Sketching" (Waleed).
  • Additional Question Compilation (Google Doc).
  • Opsimath's Solutions (Cleaned version).
  • Mock Interviews on YouTube: Cambridge, Oxford, Jaymin Shah, Jesus College, Tom Rocks Maths.
  • STEP Support Webinars run annually before interview season.
  • Daily Integral (Stick to Easy/Medium).

AEA

  • Past Papers are the main resource. Uses normal spec but harder questions.

STEP

  • STEP Support Programme: The best way to bridge the gap from A Level.
  • STEP Question Bank: Great for doing individual questions rather than full 3-hour papers.
  • Integral Maths: Detailed solutions (User: mei-stepaea, Pass: Stepaea1).
  • Vishal Gupta's Website has good advice.
  • Maths 505 and Michael Penn can help with harder integration, though some content may be beyond scope (e.g., contour integration).
