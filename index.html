About StepBot3

This was completely vibe coded. I don't know how to do any of ts.

Features

  • Question Database: STEP 2 and 3 from 2008 to 2024. More questions will be added if I can be bothered, but this is unlikely.
  • Practice Mode: Filter questions by paper, type, and topic. You can track your progress in the sidebar, which filters based on selected topics.
  • Mock Mode: Generate timed mock papers consisting of 12 questions you have not done. Grade boundaries interpolated from years.
  • Progress Tracking: Marks questions as complete, logs your scores, and saves your notes automatically to your browser.
  • Mark Schemes: View PDF mark schemes within the app.

Note: All data is stored locally on your device. Clearing your browser cache will remove your progress.

Resources & Tips

Edexcel A Level Maths:

●     Past paper spam is the best way to revise maths. There are ridiculous amounts of past papers, both official ones and community ones. I’ve just attached the PMT page for Edexcel A Level Maths from 2017 onwards, but it’s so easy to find more if you run out. Do these in timed conditions whenever possible, then review your mistake using the mark scheme. Some more sources off the top of my head include Crash Maths and Madas Maths but as I said these are essentially endless. Neil Does Maths is a bit of a niche Youtube channel, but it’s essentially just a bloke going through past paper questions. This could be good if you want to use past paper questions but struggle to understand markschemes.

●     The A Level Maths textbooks are pretty good. Unlike other subjects, you can actually use them to learn the subject. I find that the questions at the back of each chapter are okay, but it might just be better to use past paper questions, since these can be too easy, too hard, or just unrealistic questions. Also note there are occasional mistakes in the textbooks. Pete Hart has made videos going through all the exercises for the textbooks which are a good accompaniment to the textbooks. He’s also a great guy. ● TLMaths is a Youtube channel which has a playlist teaching everything in A Level Maths with example questions and such. If you needed to learn a topic as fast as possible this would be the best way; there are not as many details as other channels and past paper questions are not used, but it is completely comprehensive. You may wish to use his spreadsheet to find out which videos are relevant for your exam board. Bicen Maths is probably the most popular maths Youtube channel for good reason, it covers pretty much everything in great detail; his stuff is probably the best way to learn from videos. Haberdashers' Adams Maths Department is another channel which seems to have gone through the whole course. I have not watched their videos and they look a bit more like the 2010 style of teaching videos - not that this is necessarily a bad thing. It’s an option!

●     Madas Maths focuses more on the resources available on their website than their Youtube channel, but both are available. I have not used Madas much personally, however I hear great things about their custom papers, particularly if aiming for top grades. They also appear to have comprehensive powerpoints for teaching the whole course. Similarly, Dr. Frost has powerpoints going through everything in the course, which are especially useful for mechanics in my experience. You could absolutely teach yourself the course through Dr. Frost’s resources in my experience.

 

Edexcel A Level Further Maths:

●     Past paper spam again will be the best way to revise. I’ve only attached the PMT link but there are heaps of further maths papers available if you go looking. It might be a good shout to make a spreadsheet of which ones you have completed. Just as for normal maths, Neil Does Maths has some good videos going through some exam questions but there are much fewer of these than for normal maths.

●     Regarding textbooks, it’s again exactly the same things to say as the normal A Level Maths textbooks. Note that I only have PDFs for the core pure textbooks as well as Further Mechanics 1 and Further Stats 1.

●     TLMaths has not quite completed his videos for all of further maths - I think that’s a

work in progress? Bicen Maths probably has completed the course knowing the amount of videos on there but I haven’t personally used it. As for other channels teaching the whole course, there is also FM Videos which is one of those channels which is a bit more 2010 style, but it’s probably still fine. Having a quick look, it really seems they have stuff for like every exam board which is lovely and whimsical.

●     Madas Maths does have some stuff for further, but I don’t think it’s as complete as for the normal spec. As for Dr. Frost, I have only used his resources for mechanics when it comes to further maths, but they were very useful in that case.

 

TMUA, MAT, Senior Maths Challenge, and Senior Kangaroo: (note: did not take MAT)

●     R2Drew2 is the goat for TMUA - he has videos going through every past paper including essentially every community past paper. He also has similarly exquisite videos for many senior maths challenge papers and some senior kangaroo ones. He also has some videos going through stuff which is unique to the TMUA specification or which you might not have considered in quite a while, such as quadrilaterals.

●     As for TMUA past papers, you should be aiming to do all of them at least once - there’s really not that many official ones, and given that it is the most important part of your application for many universities you really should put in the work. Please note that older papers are much easier!! Do not think you are chilling if you get an 8.0 or something on the 2017 paper, because you may be humbled by newer papers. Then, once you have done all of the existing past papers, you have probably done enough. But if you really want to go for a great grade, then you may wish to do some of the many TMUA community past papers; these are just unofficial TMUA mock papers. I should comment on the difficulty of some of these:

○     The papers by Tyler Tutoring, which are four of each, are probably slightly easier than the real paper.

○ The papers by Yotta are absolutely diabolical and they will humble you. They are meant to be harder than a real paper.

○ The papers by James are quite a bit easier than the real TMUA - I don’t think they’re worth spending too much time on.

○ The papers by Asher Falcon are also quite a bit easier.

○ The papers by Beyond Horizon are probably a touch harder than the real paper.

○ I haven’t done the papers by Zetta or Oxbridge Applications so can’t really comment on their difficulty.

○ There is also a MEGA folder with two papers by STEPMaths which I couldn’t be bothered to add to Google Drive.

○ There is also a 2024 Chinese TMUA Paper which consists of questions recalled by students after the 2024 exam.

●     MAT past papers are of course relevant to MAT but also to TMUA. I didn’t take MAT so I don’t know of any community MAT papers but I’m sure they exist. Senior maths challenge past papers are essentially the only way to revise for the maths challenge since it’s such a unique exam. R2Drew2 has solution videos for many of them. I suppose Senior Kangaroo past papers are also relevant to the maths challenge since it’s essentially the same thing - I guess ‘unique’ was a bit of a stretch.

●     The MAT livestreams are I suppose the best preparation for the MAT after past papers, again speaking as someone who did not sit MAT - but it is the official resource run by Oxford for students preparing to sit MAT.

●     Unfortunately, the Cambridge CS Applicant Discord does actually have some good stuff in it, which is regrettable because it is Discord. I would recommend just lurking rather than talking because if you are talking in this Discord server you surely smell.

●     The Siklos book intends to prepare students for university entrance exams including TMUA. I don’t think it’s that exceptional, especially for TMUA, but some have had a good experience with it. The STEP, MAT, TMUA textbook is essentially the same thing however I have genuinely not heard anybody use it and I don’t think it’s a very good use of time to be honest.

 

BMO: (note: flopped this lol)

●     Past paper spam is the best way to go, but then again the papers are three and a half hours so this is quite difficult! Like STEP, the longer nature of the questions might be more suited to doing one or two questions at a time and maybe leaving a paper or two for the last days before the exam. However, remember that qualifying to BMO2 is very hard! It might be worth revising other, more relevant areas rather than BMO1 unless you are already close to the boundary for BMO2. In this case though, there are also literal decades of BMO1 and BMO2 questions with video solutions on the UKMT website. If somehow you do run out or something, then essentially any Olympiad questions or papers from any country will also be fine. You may wish to find examples of these from channels like Michael Penn who often cover Olympiad problems.

 

Oxbridge Interviews:

●     This Google Drive contains absolutely every mock interview and past interview question you will need. Due to the nature of interviews, there are no official past papers or anything, but there are many community made mock interview resources, which are compiled in the Google Drive. My strategy was to print out all of the following and keep them in a folder so that I could gradually chip away at the questions and make some tangible progress. Out of these, the following were the most useful to me:

○     The Oxford interview questionnaires, handwritten notes compiled by people immediately after their interviews, and sheets with headings like ‘Interview 1’ and ‘Interview 2’ are, as the name suggests, peoples’ actual interview questions - therefore, you should do them!

STEP:

●     The STEP support programme has modules to help students prepare from STEP, building up from A Level Maths. I haven’t used it too much but I’ve heard great things, and it seems to be the best way to bridge the gap from A Level to STEP.

●     Using past papers is a little bit more difficult for STEP than other exams as it is three hours, so doing past papers in timed conditions is quite hard to do efficiently. Therefore, consider using the STEP question bank website to do individual questions at a time, though do consider leaving a few papers for timed conditions near the time of the exam. There are a few community papers for STEP, namely a mock paper and a ‘STEP 4’ paper, which is supposed to be a little harder than STEP 3, but in my experience is of similar difficulty. Finally, you may choose to use the solutions from Integral Maths which are more detailed than official solutions. To login, use the username mei- stepaea and the password Stepaea1.

●     The Siklos book is more suited to STEP than other exams like TMUA, but in my opinion it’s more worth it to just use the STEP Support Programme. Similarly, the STEP, MAT, TMUA textbook… exists - I really don’t see the point though.

●     Vishal Gupta’s website has some good advice for STEP.

●     As for Youtube channels which are not specifically for STEP but which may be useful, Maths 505 and Michael Penn could help with the harder integration questions in STEP. However, they are both definitely not aimed at the knowledge of STEP students and thus some of their videos may be inaccessible, such as using contour integration.
